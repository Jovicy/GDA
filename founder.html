Our Founder

  • Mr. Kassoum Coulibaly is an economist, a strategic advisor, a human rights and pan-Africanism activist, an intellectual and social entrepreneur motivated and committed for global economic progress, global sustainable development, and the promotion of universal peace with over 25 years of working experience with youth and women in Africa and around the world. He now dedicates his entire life to the promotion and protection of human rights and women's and girls' rights in particular, health, education, entrepreneurship, gender equality, and women's empowerment, prevention and response to gender-based violence (GBV), and child protection through the engagement of men and boys worldwide.
  • Mr. Kassoum Coulibaly is the Co-Founder and President of the Groupe du Développement de l’Afrique (GDA), the Afrika Global Gender Academy (AGGA), and the Observatoire des Violences Basées sur le Genre (OVBGA). He is a member of the MenEngage Global Alliance, National Coordinator of the MenEngage Network Mali, and former Vice-President of the Steering Committee of the MenEngage Africa Alliance. He is also a member of the UN Women’s HeForShe Initiative and Co-Founder of the Alliance for Positive Masculinity in Africa (AMPA), the African Mining Alliance (AMA), the Alliance for African Renaissance (ARA), and the African Youth Center (CAJ). Since 2020, Mr. Kassoum Coulibaly is also an Expert Member of the World Economic Forum (WEF) in Davos, Switzerland.
  • He has worked and continues to work as an International Expert with several cooperation agencies, national and international NGOs, and United Nations and International Organizations such as UN Women, UNFPA, UNICEF, UNESCO, UNDP, UN Volunteers Programme, UNCTAD, the CONFEJ ES, the International Council for Small Business (ICSB), HELVETAS Switzerland, Lux-Development, CECI Canada, the Canadian and Malian Red Cross, in Mali, Africa, and worldwide. He has been a very active member of renowned global youth organizations such as the International Association of Students in Economics and Commerce (AIESEC International) and Junior Chamber International (JCI), where he held leadership positions. As an Economic Freedom Fighter in Africa (CLEA), Mr. Coulibaly has also been supporting the promotion of economic, social, and cultural entrepreneurship for more than ten (10) years in youth and women's organizations in the fields of Agriculture, Crafts, Industry, Commerce, etc... through information and communication, education and training, technical assistance and consulting, studies, and research.
  • In 2011-2012, Mr. Kassoum Coulibaly served as the Economic and Commercial Counselor for Africa at the Sichuan Supplier Chamber of Commerce (SSCC) in the People's Republic of China and continues to contribute to the development of economic, social, and cultural relations between Africa and China. In 2015-2016, he was selected as one of UN Women's Global Champions for Women's Economic Empowerment (WEE), where he demonstrated his ability to support rural women entrepreneurs. In 2016-2017, he was one of the mentors of UN Women's program for women's economic empowerment within the Business Development Group (BDG). In 2018, he joined the Side by Side Movement for Gender Justice as a key member of its national steering committee in Mali, the sub-regional steering committee in West Africa, and the global steering committee in the United Kingdom. In 2019, he was appointed as a Member and Reporter of the Advisory Council of Civil Society (ACCS) of UN Women in Mali. In 2020, he coordinated the Support Program for the Development of Small and Medium Enterprises (PAD-PME) in Mali.
  • Mr. Kassoum Coulibaly holds a Diploma of General University Studies (DEUG) in Economics and Development from the University of Bouaké (Côte d'Ivoire), as well as a Bachelor's and Master's degree in Management Sciences from the University of Bamako (Mali) in 2000. He also obtained a Postgraduate Diploma in Human Rights (Specializing in Economic, Social, and Cultural Rights) from the Henry Dunant University Center (CUHD) in Geneva, Switzerland, in 2008, a Certificate in Mining Policy and Contract Negotiation from the University of Johannesburg (UJ) in South Africa in 2013, and a Certificate in Business and Human Rights in 2016, and a Higher Certificate in Human Rights on the role of men and boys in achieving gender equality and women's empowerment (Positive Masculinities) in 2018 at the University of Pretoria (UP) in South Africa. Finally, he obtained in 2020, the prestigious International GenderPro Certificate from the Global Women's Institute (GWI) at the George Washington University (GWU) in the United States of America (USA).
  • Mr. Kassoum Coulibaly speaks, reads, and writes fluently in Bamanankan, French, and English.
  • He is the author and co-author of several publications including: Word Grenier "Bamanan", Working Document, IIEDH - University of Fribourg - 2008 Cultural Rights and Treatment of Violence, Editions - Editions L’Harmattan - 2009 Guide to Promoting Volunteering in Southern Countries, CONFEJES - 2010 Anthropology of Human Rights among the Bamanan of Mali, UE Editions - 2011
  • Throughout his life, Mr. Kassoum Coulibaly has defended the following values:
  • Striving for excellence
  • Sustainable leadership
  • Living in diversity
  • Promoting action leadership
  • Demonstrating integrity
  • Loving participation.
